CSS Display Properties

Now, we will learn about CSS's display properties. This tutorial covers some of the major and widely used display properties.
Display: Inline
We have learned about the elements like div which are block elements. But what if you want to make block element act as inline use as much space as required and not the whole line. This is where Inline property comes in. Example is shown below:
<div class="box">
I am a box
</div>
<div class="box"> and also a nice box.</div>
<span>I am a good</span>
<span> boy.</span>
 Output:
I am a box
and also a nice box.
I am a good boy.
Display: Inline-block
We noticed in above examlple that giving padding and margin to the box class gave padding to the text from all sides except from top which made other texts come within the border of the the texts - "I am a box, and also a nice box"; and same case is with applying margins. To rectify such issues we give Inline-block property to the element. Example is shown below:
<div class="box box0">
I am a box
</div>
<div class="box"> and also a nice box.</div>
<span>I am a good</span>
<span> boy.</span>
 Output:
I am a box
and also a nice box.
I am a good boy.
Display: none and visibility: hidden
Answer these two questions:-
1. Do you want that thing not to be there?
2.Do you want to leave some space left for that?
if the answer to 1st is yes and 2nd is no, then none is the property you are looking for. Else, if your answer to both of them is yes, then you should go with hidden visibility property. Let's see this with an example:-
<div class="box10 box1">
I am a box
</div>
<div class="box10"> and also a nice box.</div>
<span>I am a good</span>
<span> boy.</span>
 Output(Display: none):
I am a box
and also a nice box.
I am a good boy.
<div class="box10 box2">
I am a box
</div>
<div class="box10"> and also a nice box.</div>
<span>I am a good</span>
<span> boy.</span>
 Output(Display: none):
I am a box
and also a nice box.
I am a good boy.
